    2:00 PM - 3:20 PM | NEGOTIATION 1
    Faculty: Nir Halevy

    Negotiations take place in organizations on a daily basis. Therefore, effective managers and leaders should be familiar with the strategy and psychology of conflict and negotiate effectively with other persons, departments, organizations, and stakeholders. Hence, the first aim of our two sessions is to develop your ability to analyze negotiation situations. In addition to understanding how to analyze negotiation situations, to negotiate effectively, you must have a broad repertoire of behavioral skills that can be applied to the various negotiation situations you are likely to encounter. Therefore, a second aim of the sessions is to allow you to experience a negotiation situation by playing a role in simulated negotiation. Our exercise will allow you to try out tactics that might feel uncomfortable trying in an actual negotiation, get constructive feedback from your counterparts and classmates, and learn how you come across.

    During our first session, we will introduce core concepts related to deal-making negotiation and discuss how to prepare for a negotiation. You will then receive role materials for a simulated negotiation with one of the other participants who will act in the complementary role. You will have time to prepare for the New Recruit negotiation during our session. You will submit your detailed agreement form for the New Recruit negotiation exercise by the end of our first session to facilitate smooth debriefs and discussions following the exercise in our second session. Your active and conscientious participation in the negotiation is a crucial part of our sessions -- how much you and other participants learn about negotiation depends upon how well you play your assigned role. We will have interesting data to examine only if everyone carries their role in the simulated negotiation diligently.

    New Recruit Exercise

    (preparation and negotiation conducted in class)

    In this session we will:

    • Introduce foundational concepts (e.g., ZOPA, BATNA) in negotiation
    • Discuss how to prepare for a negotiation
    • Discuss the role of power, information asymmetry, trust, and information exchange in deal-making negotiations;
    • Prepare for and negotiate the New Recruit Exercise


    3:20 PM - 3:40 PM | BREAK (faculty discretion; times are approximate)


    3:40 PM - 5:00 PM | NEGOTIATION 2

    Faculty: Nir Halevy

    During this session we will debrief the New Recruit negotiation exercise based on your submitted agreements, and discuss important processes, including distinguishing issue types in negotiation, power in negotiation, value creation and value claiming strategies, and subjective value in negotiation. I will ask you challenging questions about the tactics you employed during the negotiation and ask that you share your thinking process with the other participants so that we all learn together from everyone’s failures and successes.

     In this session we will:

    • Debrief your New Recruit negotiation
    • Discuss methods for crafting mutually beneficial, high-value deals, including drafting contingent contracts
    • Discuss different aspects of subjective value in negotiation (such as satisfaction with the outcomes, the process, the self, and the relationship with your counterpart)
    • Discuss how using different communication technologies influences negotiation processes and outcomes
