    3:40 AM - 5:00 PM | Marketing 2
    Faculty: Jim Lattin

    Positioning

    The case describes several chapters of the story of the founding of Lumo Bodytech (including the formation of the team, identifying an idea to work on, launching a product, pivoting to a new platform, funding the business, and learning how to hire and fire). For the purposes of our discussion, we will focus on the Lumo Back. In reading the case, focus your attention on the material from Finding an Idea and (Potentially) Losing a Cofounder on page 4 to Lessons Learned and a Second Try: The Lumo Lift on page 7. Then answer the discussion questions posed below.

    Reading(s):

    • Case: Lumo Bodytech: A Bumpy Journey (Stanford E-645)

    Preparation Question(s):

    1. Based on the information available in the case, who do you think is the best target customer for Lumo Bodytech? Why? If you wanted to describe the persona for this target customer, what additional information would you want to have? How would you get it?
    2. Given your choice of target customer in Question 1 above, develop a positioning statement for the Lumo Back (assume that the product is the one made available on Kickstarter in August 2012). Make sure your statement clearly communicates WHO the product is for, WHAT benefits the product delivers, and either HOW it is different from existing alternatives or WHY customers should feel compelled to choose Lumo Back. Be prepared to share this statement with other Ignite participants during class.
    3. What did you think of the process the co-founders went through to understand consumers and refine their product concept? Did you have any concerns? Was there anything missing?
    4. Given the market response to the Lumo Back, what would your next steps have been? Why?

    2:00 PM - 3:20 PM| Marketing 3
    Faculty: Jim Lattin

    Go-To-Market Strategy

    In May 2001, Aqualisa launched the Quartz shower, a significant product innovation in the U.K. shower market. By September 2001, the euphoria surrounding the product's initial launch had long since faded. Harry Rawlinson, managing director of Aqualisa, knew that the Quartz was technologically leaps and bounds above other U.K. showers in terms of water pressure, ease of installation, use, and design. But for some reason, it simply wasn't selling.

    Reading(s):

    • Case: Aqualisa Quartz: Simply a Better Shower

    Preparation Question(s):

    1. How does Aqualisa go to market with its conventional product line (i.e., the Aquavalve 609 mixer shower plus pump)?
    2. What is the problem with the Aqualisa Quartz shower? Why isnâ€™t it selling?
    3. Is the Aqualisa Quartz a new product that creates any value? If so, for whom?
    4. Suggest a different go-to-market strategy for the Aqualisa Quartz. How does your strategy address the problems you identified? What do you think are the main risks/challenges of such a strategy?

    Effective Entrepreneurial Communication: Being Pitch Perfect 

    Warren Buffett refuses to invest in companies that he cannot understand with just a short description or conversation. Your team has one opportunity to give a convincing pitch. What will distinguish you from the rest? Will your team stand apart as cohesive, confident, enthusiastic and distinct? Or will you leave behind a less favorable impression?

We will discuss ways to get your proposal across persuasively, to work smoothly as a team, and to develop visuals that quickly support your message. You will also receive instructions on your subsequent videotaped team presentation rehearsal.

    9:40 AM - 12:40 PM | Design Thinking Workshop | Z301 (GSB Map)

    Faculty: Sarah Soule

    Stanford is one of the principal educational institutions driving the paradigm shift in the design field today. This overview session is designed to teach you about design thinking â€” a human-centered, prototype-driven process for innovation that can be applied to products, services, and experiences. In the session, you will not only learn about the power of design thinking to help you with creative problem-solving, you will also learn about its power to foster innovative thinking among individuals practicing the process. Specifically, you will be taken through a set of exercises that are tied to your team's idea, and designed to get you to both uncover unmet needs of the user of your product, service, or experience. You will also be taught some basics about team dynamics, and rapid prototyping of ideas.

    Important note: Teams must complete the prep work below BEFORE our session. Instructions will be released after teams are formed, and teams will have from June 26- June 29 to complete this assignment.

    To get the most out of our time together, it is essential that you complete the prep work:

    Reading(s):

    • Reading: Design Thinking (HBR)
    • Handout: Empathy Logbook (shared in class on Monday, June 26)

    Preparation:

    1. Look around and familiarize yourself with the wealth of resources at the Stanford d.school website.
